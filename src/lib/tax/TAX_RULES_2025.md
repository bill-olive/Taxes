### American Opportunity Tax Credit (AOTC)

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Maximum credit | $2,500 |
| Calculation | 100% of first $2,000 + 25% of next $2,000 in qualified expenses |
| Refundable portion | 40% of credit (up to $1,000) |
| MAGI phase-out start (single) | $80,000 |
| MAGI phase-out end (single) | $90,000 |
| Maximum years claimable | 4 tax years per student |

- **Source:** IRC § 25A(b), (d), and (i)
- **Note:** AOTC amounts and phase-out thresholds are statutory (set by the American Taxpayer Relief Act of 2012) and are **not indexed for inflation**. The same thresholds apply for 2025 as for prior years.
- **URL:** https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/individuals/aotc
- **Form:** IRS Form 8863, Education Credits

## Georgia State Tax Rules

### Georgia Income Tax Rate (2025)

| Rate | Applies To |
|---|---|
| **5.39% flat rate** | All Georgia taxable income |

- **Source:** Georgia HB 1015 (2024 legislative session), signed into law April 2024
- **Legal Basis:** O.C.G.A. § 48-7-20, as amended by HB 1015
- **Note:** Georgia transitioned from a graduated bracket system (1%–5.49% across six brackets) to a single flat rate effective January 1, 2025. The rate is scheduled to decrease by 0.10% per year, contingent on state revenue triggers, with a floor of 4.99%.
- **URL:** https://dor.georgia.gov/income-tax
- **Previous (2024):** Graduated brackets from 1% to 5.49% (six brackets, top bracket above $7,000)

### Georgia Standard Deduction — Single Filers

| Filing Status | 2024 Amount | 2025 Amount |
|---|---|---|
| Single / Head of Household | $5,400 | **$12,000** |

- **Source:** Georgia HB 1015 (2024), amending O.C.G.A. § 48-7-20
- **Note:** HB 1015 more than doubled the standard deduction as part of the flat tax transition.

### Georgia Personal Exemption

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Taxpayer exemption | $3,700 |
| Dependent exemption | $3,000 per dependent |

- **Source:** O.C.G.A. § 48-7-26
- **Note:** Personal exemptions were **not eliminated** by HB 1015 for the 2025 tax year.

### Summary of Georgia 2024 → 2025 Changes

| Item | 2024 | 2025 | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rate structure | Graduated (1%–5.49%) | Flat 5.39% | Simplified |
| Standard deduction (single) | $5,400 | $12,000 | +$6,600 |
| Personal exemption | $3,700 | $3,700 | No change |
| Top marginal rate | 5.49% | 5.39% | -0.10% |

## Property-Related Rules

### Property Tax Deduction (Federal)

Property taxes paid are deductible as part of the SALT (State and Local Tax) deduction, subject to the $10,000 combined cap.

- **Source:** IRC § 164(a)(1) (property tax deductibility), IRC § 164(b)(6) (SALT cap)
- **Note:** The SALT cap combines state income tax, local income tax, and property tax. Total deduction across all three cannot exceed $10,000.

### HOA Fees and Homeowner's Insurance

| Expense | Deductible? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| HOA / Condo fees | **No** | Not deductible for a primary residence |
| Homeowner's insurance | **No** | Not deductible for a primary residence |

- **Source:** IRS Publication 530 (Tax Information for Homeowners)
- **URL:** https://www.irs.gov/publications/p530
- **Note:** These expenses may be deductible for rental properties or home offices, but not for a standard primary residence.

### Mortgage Interest Deduction
